About Me

I completed my Ph.D. from the Statistics Department at Columbia University, New York. I was jointly advised by Prof. Bodhisattva Sen and Prof. Sumit Mukherjee. I did my Bachelors and Masters in Statistics from Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata.

I was the inaugural PIMS Kantorovich Initiative Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of British Columbia, Vancouver (2022–23), jointly advised by Prof. Young-Heon Kim, Prof. Soumik Pal, and Prof. Geoffrey Schiebinger. Starting July 2023, I joined Chicago Booth as an Assistant Professor in the Econometrics and Statistics group.

Research

  • Nonparametric inference and testing
  • Optimal transport and measure transportation
  • Generative modeling via PDE/OT (e.g., Sinkhorn, mirror flows)
  • Inference for dependent data (Ising/graphical models)
  • U-statistics, large deviations, and asymptotics
  • Causal inference
